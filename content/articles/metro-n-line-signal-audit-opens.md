The Metropolis Transit Authority opened a formal signal and intercom audit for the N-Line subway after riders filed more than four hundred complaints in nine days about station announcements naming stops that were not next on the schedule.

Technicians duplicated several faults in a test train Wednesday morning; agency leadership said no safety-critical braking systems were implicated but promised nightly public logs until the review ends.

Planet reporters correlated complaints to a three-mile tunnel segment under the garment district where fiber backbone contracts were renewed last autumn.
